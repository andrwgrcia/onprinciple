# ON PRINCIPLE
## Brief Standards
### Version 1 — April 2026

*The operational framework governing all Briefs produced by On Principle*

## EDITORIAL DISCLAIMER

Every Brief produced by On Principle carries the following disclaimer at the top:

"This Brief is a research synthesis produced by applying the On Principle Core Beliefs framework to submitted content. It is not original editorial commentary. It is not the opinion of any individual author. It is what the stated framework produces when applied to the submitted content. The Core Beliefs framework that governs this analysis is published and versioned at github.com/andrwgrcia/onprinciple."

## THE VALUE FRAMEWORK

All analysis is evaluated against the Core Beliefs, the published value framework governing this project. The current version is maintained at github.com/andrwgrcia/onprinciple. That document is the authority. These Standards reference it but do not reproduce it. When the Core Beliefs are updated, the Briefs produced after that update reflect the new version. The version number in effect at the time of each Brief is recorded with that Brief.

---

## SOURCING REQUIREMENTS

All claims must be verifiable from primary sources. Primary sources include federal and state legislation, congressional and legislative voting records, government agency data, court opinions and decisions, Federal Register publications, official government reports, and on-the-record statements by named officials. Secondary sources may be used to locate primary sources but are not themselves sufficient. Wikipedia is not a source. Advocacy math and unsourced statistics are rejected regardless of whether the source is aligned with the framework.

Legislation must be named precisely — bill number, sponsor, and chamber — so readers can search and verify independently. Federal Register citations must include volume, page number, and date. When a figure or claim originates from a named institution's published data, that institution and publication must be identified.

When a source cannot be independently verified, it may still be used if its provenance is credible, but the inability to verify independently must be disclosed explicitly.

## PARTISAN DRIFT CHECK

Before any Brief is finalized, the analysis is checked for partisan drift in both directions — toward a tribal position the Core Beliefs do not support, and away from a legitimate conclusion the Core Beliefs do support.

## BRIEF STRUCTURE

Every Brief contains four sections in this order. No other sections. No preamble between the disclaimer and Section 1.

**Section 1 — The Situation**

Factual background only. No analysis. No opinion. No interpretive framing. Primary sources only, as defined above. If a fact cannot be verified, it does not go in. Uncertainty must be disclosed explicitly rather than papered over.

**Section 2 — Beliefs Application**

Identify which Core Beliefs apply to the submitted content and explain specifically how each one applies. This is framework application, not editorial opinion. Where a belief creates tension or pulls in more than one direction, both directions must be noted. Where the framework arrives at a position that conflicts with current law or constitutional interpretation, that conflict must be acknowledged honestly and labeled as a position rather than settled fact.

**Section 3 — Bottom Line**

A single synthesis of what the framework concludes. This is the logical product of Section 2 applied to the facts in Section 1. It is not anyone's opinion. It is what the stated framework produces when applied to the established facts.

**Section 4 — What to Watch**

What comes next that could change the analysis. Pending votes, legal challenges, enforcement responses, legislative timelines, court decisions in similar jurisdictions, or other developments that would materially affect the framework's conclusions.

---

## WHEN SUBMITTED CONTENT INVOLVES MULTIPLE DISTINCT MEASURES

If the submitted content involves two or more legally or structurally distinct measures, Section 2 must be subdivided — one subsection per measure — so the framework is applied separately to each. Section 1 and Section 4 may be combined across measures when they share factual background or a common timeline. Section 3 is a single synthesis across all measures.

---

## WRITING STANDARDS

Every Brief must work as both written text and audio.

Short declarative sentences. No bullet points. No numbered lists inside the body of the Brief. No symbols. Clear paragraph breaks. Plain language. Drama carried by plainly stated facts, not by narrative scaffolding or adjectives.

---

## WHAT A BRIEF IS NOT

A Brief is not editorial commentary. It is not the opinion of any individual. It is not advocacy. If the framework arrives at a conclusion that appears to align with one party or one side of a debate, that is the framework's product — the result of applying stated principles consistently to established facts.
